1.Jasmine

Madurai Jasmine, also known as Madurai Malli is a type of jasmine flower that is native to the Indian state of Tamilnadu. It is a highly fragrant flower that is widely used in India and all over the world for various purposes, including religious rituals, decoration, and perfumery. The Madurai Jasmine flower has small, white, star-shaped petals that bloom in clusters. The flower’s fragrance is sweet and intense, with a slightly fruity undertone. The flower blooms during the summer months, from April to June, and is harvested early in the morning before the sun rises.

In India, Madurai Jasmine is considered a symbol of purity, love, and devotion. It is often used in wedding ceremonies, garlands, and other religious rituals. The flower is also widely used in Ayurvedic medicine for its various healing properties. Madurai Jasmine is an important crop in the Tamil Nadu region of India, and many small farmers depend on its cultivation for their livelihoods. The flower is also exported to other countries, where it is used in perfumes, essential oils, and other beauty products. Overall, the Madurai Jasmine flower is a beloved and important part of Indian culture and is valued for its beauty, fragrance, and cultural significance.

2.Jasmine Angustifolium

Madurai Jasminum angustifolium is a variety of jasmine plant that is also known as Madurai Mullai in India. It is a type of shrub that is native to the southern regions of India, particularly in the state of Tamil Nadu, where it is widely cultivated and valued for its fragrant flowers. The Madurai Jasminum angustifolium plant has narrow, pointed leaves that grow in pairs along its stem. It produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that are highly fragrant and bloom in clusters during the summer months.


The flowers of Madurai Jasminum angustifolium are used for a variety of purposes in India. They are commonly used in religious rituals, such as in garlands for temples or in offerings to deities. They are also used in traditional medicine for their various healing properties, including as an anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and for treating skin conditions. In addition, the flowers of Madurai Jasminum angustifolium are highly valued in the perfume industry for their sweet and floral scent. The essential oil extracted from the flowers is used in perfumes, soaps, and other cosmetic products. Overall, Madurai Jasminum angustifolium is an important plant in India, both culturally and economically. It is a symbol of purity, love, and devotion, and is valued for its beauty and fragrance.
